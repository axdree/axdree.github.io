About DISM and Career Prospects

About DISM

Course Description

The Diploma in Infocomm Security Management (DISM) course in Singapore Polytechnic provides students with a holistic approach towards Cyber Security.

Through its carefully crafted programs and modules and its dynamic style of teaching, Students would feel confidently equipped to enter the challenging infocomm security.

Curriculum

DISM offers an interesting curriculum that covers offensive attacks, defensive methods and investigative skills which include the important areas of security technology, security management, information technology, law and teamwork and communications.

DISM's Modules include but are not limited to:
Communicating for Professional Effectiveness, Critical and Analytical Thinking, Linux Administration and Security, Front-End Web Development, Infocomm Security, Computer Law and Investigation, Ethical Hacking and Defences, Secure Coding and Digital Forensics and Investigation

Career Prospects DISM

Cyber Security Analyst

Cyber Security Analysts plan and carry out security measures to protect a company’s computer networks and systems.

They keep constant tabs on threats and monitor their organization’s networks for any breaches in security.

A typical cyber security analyst job description includes installing firewall and encryption tools, reporting breaches or weak spots, researching IT trends, educating the rest of the company on security—and even simulating security attacks to find potential vulnerabilities.

Forensics Investigator

A Computer Forensics Investigator or Forensic Analyst is a specially trained professional who works with law enforcement agencies, as well as private firms, to retrieve information from computers and other types of data storage devices.

During investigations, a forensics investigator recovers and examines data from computers and other electronic storage devices in order to use the data as evidence. When equipment is damaged, the investigator must dismantle and rebuild the system in order to recover lost data.

Cyber Security Engineer

Cyber Security Engineers identify threats and vulnerabilities in systems and software, then apply their skills to developing and implementing high-tech solutions to defend against hacking, malware and ransomware, insider threats and all types of cybercrime.

A Cyber Security Engineer's job roles include developing/ engineering secure, trusted systems, performing assessments and penetration testing, managing security technology and audit/intrusion systems and developing and fielding secure network solutions to protect against advanced persistent threats.

Systems Administrator

System Administrators are responsible for the maintenance, configuration, and reliable operation of computer systems and servers. They install hardware and software, and participate in research and development to continuously improve and keep up with the IT business needs of their organization.

System administrators also actively resolve problems and issues with computer and server systems to limit work disruptions within the company.
